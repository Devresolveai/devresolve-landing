How it works

Four steps. Zero manual work.

DevResolve plugs into your existing Slack and Jira setup. No new workflows to build. No tickets to copy-paste.

01

Ticket goes to Done

A Jira webhook fires the moment an engineer marks a ticket resolved. DevResolve wakes up instantly.

02

Slack thread is harvested

The full resolution thread is fetched from Slack — every message, timestamp, and participant captured.

03

Resolution is synthesized

DevResolve's AI engine reads the thread and produces a structured resolution document: root cause, action steps, affected systems, and a reusability score.

04

Jira is updated & closed

Public resolution goes to the customer portal. Technical root cause stays internal. Ticket transitions to Done automatically.

Features

Built for engineering teams who hate toil

Strict audience separation

Customer-facing resolution and internal engineering notes are generated, stored, and delivered as two completely separate artifacts. They are enforced at the delivery layer — mixing is architecturally impossible.

Structured resolution synthesis

DevResolve's AI engine extracts root cause, resolution steps, affected systems, environment signals, and a reusability score from your team's existing conversation — structured output, not a text summary.

Ticket deflection engine

Every resolved incident builds your deflection library. When a similar issue comes in, DevResolve surfaces the proven resolution automatically — reducing repeat tickets without adding agent headcount.

Full incident timeline

Every resolution document includes a timestamped incident timeline — when the issue started, when the team engaged, when the fix landed. A complete record without anyone writing a post-mortem.

Human-in-the-loop review

Every resolution routes through a team review step before anything reaches the customer. Approve it as-is or edit both the customer message and the internal notes independently — then send with one click.

Ticketing-system agnostic

Jira is fully supported today. Additional platforms are in the pipeline. DevResolve's resolution engine is platform-neutral — the same AI output routes to whichever ticketing system your team uses.

Resilient by design

DevResolve is built to stay online under load. AI requests retry automatically on transient failures with progressive back-off. Your incident pipeline doesn't stall because of a momentary upstream issue.

Versioned resolution history

Every resolution is stored with full version history. Re-running synthesis on the same ticket creates a new version, never an overwrite. You always have a complete audit trail of how every incident was handled.

Clean output, every time

DevResolve normalizes all AI output before it reaches your ticketing system — consistent formatting, no encoding artifacts, no rendering failures across tools. What you approve is exactly what gets delivered.

FAQ

Common questions

How does DevResolve know which conversation to use?

DevResolve automatically locates the relevant team conversation using your ticket reference. It searches across your connected channels and pulls the full thread — no manual linking or copy-pasting required.

How is the internal note guaranteed to stay private?

Internal notes and customer-facing updates are treated as two separate delivery paths from the moment they're generated. DevResolve enforces visibility at the integration layer using your ticketing platform's native access controls — not just formatting differences.

What if my team wants to edit the resolution before it goes out?

Every resolution goes through a team review step first. You can approve it as-is or open an editor that lets you update the customer message and the internal notes independently. Nothing is sent until someone on your team explicitly approves it.

What ticketing systems are supported?

Jira is fully supported today, including both cloud and self-hosted deployments. Zendesk support is in active development. DevResolve is designed to be platform-neutral — adding new integrations doesn't require changes to how resolutions are generated.

What happens if the AI output isn't usable?

DevResolve validates every AI response before it enters your workflow. If the output doesn't meet quality standards, the request is automatically retried. If it still can't produce a valid result, the ticket is flagged for manual review rather than silently failing.

Can DevResolve run in our own infrastructure?

Yes. DevResolve can be deployed within your own cloud environment or on-premises. It has no hard dependencies on external cloud services. Enterprise plans include deployment support, VPC configuration, and options for teams with strict data residency requirements.
