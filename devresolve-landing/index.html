Features

Built for engineering teams who hate toil

Strict audience separation

Customer-facing resolutions and internal engineering notes are generated, stored, and delivered as completely separate artifacts. They are enforced at the delivery layer so the two can never be mixed up.

Structured resolution synthesis

DevResolve™ extracts root cause, resolution steps, affected systems, environment signals, and a reusability score from your team's existing conversation. You get structured output, not a text summary.

Repeat ticket prevention

Every resolved incident builds your resolution library. When a similar issue comes in next time, DevResolve surfaces the proven answer automatically. You reduce repeat tickets without adding to your headcount.

Full incident timeline

Every resolution document includes a timestamped incident timeline covering when the issue started, when the team engaged, and when the fix landed. You get a complete record without anyone writing a post-mortem.

Human review built in

Every resolution goes through a team review step before anything reaches the customer. You can approve it as-is or edit the customer message and internal notes independently. Nothing ships until your team says so.

Works with any ticketing system

Jira is fully supported today and additional platforms are coming. DevResolve™ is designed to be platform-neutral so the same resolution quality routes to whichever system your team already uses.

Resilient by design

DevResolve™ is built to stay online under load. Requests retry automatically on transient failures with progressive back-off. Your incident pipeline keeps moving even when upstream services hiccup.

Full resolution history

Every resolution is stored with complete version history. Re-running on the same incident creates a new version rather than overwriting the old one. You always have a full audit trail of how every incident was handled.

Consistent output, every time

DevResolve™ normalizes all output before it reaches your ticketing system. Consistent formatting, no rendering failures across tools. What your team approves is exactly what gets delivered.

What is a Resolution?

A Resolution is any technical task successfully executed by DevResolve™ without human intervention. This includes a Jira ticket closed, a Slack incident triaged, a database sync completed, or a customer update delivered. You are only charged when the outcome is verified as complete.

Why pay per Resolution? Because we believe you should only pay for work that actually gets done. It aligns our success directly with your engineering efficiency.

FAQ

Common questions

How does DevResolve know which conversation to use?

DevResolve™ automatically locates the relevant team conversation using your ticket reference. It searches across your connected channels and pulls the full thread with no manual linking or copy-pasting required.

How is the internal note guaranteed to stay private?

Internal notes and customer-facing updates are treated as two completely separate delivery paths from the moment they are generated. DevResolve™ enforces visibility at the integration layer using your ticketing platform's native access controls rather than relying on formatting differences alone.

What if my team wants to edit the resolution before it goes out?

Every resolution goes through a team review step before anything is sent. You can approve it as-is or open an editor that lets you update the customer message and the internal notes independently. Nothing is delivered until someone on your team explicitly approves it.

What ticketing systems does DevResolve support?

Jira and Zendesk are both fully supported today. DevResolve™ is designed to be platform-neutral so adding new integrations does not require any changes to how resolutions are generated.

What happens if a resolution cannot be completed?

DevResolve™ validates every output before it enters your workflow. If the result does not meet quality standards, the request is automatically retried. If a valid result still cannot be produced, the ticket is flagged for manual review rather than failing silently. You are never charged for an incomplete resolution.

Can DevResolve run in our own infrastructure?

Yes. DevResolve™ can be deployed within your own cloud environment or on-premises. Sovereign plan customers get full BYOK support, VPC deployment, and dedicated architecture guidance for teams with strict data residency requirements.
