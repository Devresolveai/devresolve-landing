FAQ

Common questions

What kinds of tickets does DevResolve deflect?

DevResolve is purpose-built for technical customer tickets that don't require an engineer to resolve: documentation and how-to requests, configuration guidance, environment setup checks, and common troubleshooting patterns. Anything where the answer already exists in your knowledge base — docs, Jira history, Slack threads — is a candidate for automated deflection.

How does the triage and verification work?

When a ticket arrives, DevResolve doesn't just look up an answer. It also gathers environment signals and log data to verify the customer's actual setup. If the issue is a simple misconfiguration or setup error, it resolves it directly. If the logs suggest a genuine bug, it escalates with a structured report so your engineers have full context from the start, not a vague complaint.

What data sources does DevResolve synthesize from?

DevResolve synthesizes across your documentation (Confluence, Notion, GitHub wikis), your Jira ticket history, and your Slack channels. The more context it has, the higher your deflection rate. You control which sources it has access to.

Will DevResolve expose internal engineering details to customers?

No. Customer-facing responses are generated from your public-facing knowledge base and sanitized before delivery. Internal engineering context — stack traces, architecture notes, infrastructure details — is handled in a separate pipeline and never surfaces in customer replies.

Is our data used to train AI models?

No. Your data is never used to train external AI models. This is a hard commitment, documented in our Security & IP Protection policy. Sovereign Autonomy plan customers additionally get zero-data retention and BYOK, so your data never leaves your own infrastructure.

What if DevResolve can't resolve a ticket?

If the agent cannot produce a high-confidence answer, it escalates the ticket to your engineering queue but never sends a guess. When it escalates, it includes a structured triage report: what it found, what it checked, and what the likely issue is. Your engineers get context, not a blank ticket. You are never charged for unresolved tickets.

Can we deploy within our own infrastructure?

Yes. The Sovereign Autonomy plan offers full BYOK support and on-premises or VPC deployment. You bring your own LLM keys, the resolution engine runs inside your environment, and zero data ever leaves your infrastructure. Designed for teams with strict data residency or compliance requirements.
